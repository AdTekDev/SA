# Rules

## Submission Requirements

Please carefully follow the requirements below when submitting your TEST:

1. **Attendance Requirement**
   Students must be physically present in the assigned laboratory/classroom during the submission and evaluation session. Absence without permission may result in no evaluation.

2. **Submission on the System**
   The assignment must be submitted correctly on the system with all required components:

   * Part 1 → Folder "Results-screenshots"
   * Part 2 → Folder "Code-DesignPatterns"
   * Part 3 → Folder "ProjectCode"

   Missing folders or incorrect submission structure may affect your score.

3. **Evidence and Screenshot Files**
   All screenshot or evidence files must be properly named and organized clearly. File names should reflect the related task or feature for easy verification.

4. **Application Functionality**
   The most important requirement is that the application must run successfully.
   Projects that cannot be executed or demonstrated properly will receive a significant score reduction, regardless of the amount of code submitted.

5. **Oral Explanation and Evaluation**
   During the evaluation, students are expected to explain their work and answer questions from the lecturer.
   Marks will be awarded based on the parts that the student can clearly explain and demonstrate. Partial understanding will receive partial credit.

6. **Academic Integrity**
   Assignments that are identical or highly similar to another student’s work, or projects generated entirely by AI without understanding or modification, will be considered a serious academic violation. Such cases may receive zero marks or further disciplinary action.

7. **Additional Notes**

   * Students should test the application before submission.
   * Keep all source files, libraries, and configurations intact.
   * Late, incomplete, or corrupted submissions may not be accepted.
   * Students may be asked to modify or debug part of the application during the evaluation session.

Please make sure your work reflects your own understanding and effort.
